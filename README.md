# Behavioural Data Science & Behavioural AI

**Behavioural Data Science (BDS)** is the interdisciplinary field that combines behavioural science and data science to study, model, predict, and explain human, algorithmic, and systemic behaviour. It brings together psychology, economics, computer science, engineering, and philosophy to understand how people and machines actually behave — not how models assume they behave.

**Behavioural AI** applies behavioural science to the design, evaluation, and governance of artificial intelligence, so that AI systems account for how people really think, decide, and act under uncertainty — and so that the behaviour of AI systems themselves can be studied scientifically.

This organisation is the open reference hub for both disciplines: books, papers, degree programmes, short courses, chapter-by-chapter video explainers, datasets, and research materials — curated by **Professor Ganna Pogrebna**, pioneer of Behavioural Data Science and Behavioural AI.

The definitive, field-defining reference work: 44 chapters across six parts — Human Behaviour, Algorithmic Behaviour, Systems and Culture, and Applications — written by leading scholars across psychology, economics, computer science, engineering, and philosophy.

## 👩‍🔬 About the curator

**Ganna Pogrebna** is Professor at Queen's University Belfast and Senior Fellow at the Australian Strategic Policy Institute (ASPI). She is a pioneer of behavioural data science and behavioural AI, author of *The Missing B in AI* (2025) and co-editor of *The Cambridge Handbook of Behavioural Data Science* (Cambridge University Press, 2026). Her work explores how emerging technologies transform industries, customer experiences, and business models, and she advises businesses and governments on AI adoption, innovation strategy, and digital trust.
